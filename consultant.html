Be a Consultant

You’re at a crossroads in your career. You may be looking to diversify your freelance portfolio or seeking a career change after a lengthy stint in the corporate world. Perhaps you recently graduated and are trying to find a strong career fit. Exploring your options, you’ve likely come across consulting jobs. Now, you’re curious about what they entail and if you’re qualified for the role. The thing is, you can be a consultant in many different areas, and there’s no singular career path to becoming a consultant. So, there’s almost definitely a way to fit your interests and skills into a consulting role. If that sounds promising, explore the nuances of consulting, including how to find a consulting job and who’s hiring. What Is Consulting? Definition & Meaning First things first—what is consulting, exactly? Consulting is a professional service offered by individuals or firms, and it involves advising an organization on how to achieve better results. Generally, consultants possess specialized knowledge, skills, or expertise in a particular field. As a consultant, you work with clients to provide advice, strategies, and solutions to specific challenges or goals. You might leverage your extensive experience in the corporate world to resolve supply chain issues. Or, lean into your freelancing expertise to help a business level up its content. And the most attractive aspect? Consulting is not limited to one industry or sector. While each project will define the specific tasks, at its core, consulting is about delivering value. Consultants analyze existing systems or processes, identify gaps or inefficiencies, and provide actionable recommendations. You don’t just advise; you work alongside clients to implement changes and achieve desired outcomes. With a broader understanding of the term, take a look at some of the specific duties a consultant often tackles. What Is a Consultant? What Do Consultants Do? Are you still wondering, “OK, but what do consultants do all day?” Here’s a more detailed consultant definition. Whether an organization is in a rut or looking to expand, consultants can be the catalyst they need. Think of it as mentoring for an entire organization, rather than mentoring one individual. Consultants can perform various duties that vary considerably depending on the industry. With a fresh outside perspective, consultants help organizations streamline operations, increase productivity, build revenue, and make overall functions more efficient. For example, suppose a company is experiencing a high turnover in their frontline staffing. As a management consultant, you might analyze the internal communications, scheduling, and leadership styles. You can see that the frontline leaders aren’t being fully set up for success, leading to a high-stress work environment. At this point, you’d recommend a remediation plan that might include: Better training and support for managers Improved career development paths Increased staffing during peak business — Sample Consultant Job Description To help you visualize the role a bit, look through this consultant job description. The consultant will act as the principal liaison with the customer to implement best practices to drive optimized business outcomes for the customer. The consultant will be diligent, customer-focused, agile, transparent, and a strong communicator. This individual will build relationships with customers to become a trusted advisor and counsel them on solutions and recommended business practices. The successful candidate will lead an in-depth analysis of the customer’s desired business process, operations, and practices and provide recommendations for solution configuration and enhanced business practices.
